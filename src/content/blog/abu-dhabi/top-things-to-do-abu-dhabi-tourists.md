# Discover the Best of Abu Dhabi: Top 20 Things to Do

Abu Dhabi, the capital of the United Arab Emirates, is a city that seamlessly blends tradition with modernity, offering a rich tapestry of cultural experiences, stunning landmarks, and vibrant entertainment. Whether you're a first-time visitor or returning for another adventure, this guide will help you explore the top 20 things to do in Abu Dhabi.

## Landmarks

### Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque

**What It Is:** The Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque is one of the world's largest mosques and a masterpiece of Islamic architecture.

**Why It's Special:** With its 82 domes, over 1,000 columns, and gold-plated chandeliers, this mosque is a stunning fusion of Mameluke, Ottoman, and Fatimid design elements. It can accommodate over 40,000 worshippers.

- **Location:** Sheikh Rashid Bin Saeed Street, Abu Dhabi
- **Cost:** Free entry
- **Best Time to Visit:** Early morning or late afternoon for cooler temperatures and fewer crowds
- **Insider Tip:** Dress modestly. Women should bring a headscarf, and men should avoid wearing shorts. Complimentary abayas and kanduras are available for visitors.

### Qasr Al Watan

**What It Is:** A working presidential palace that offers insight into the governance and culture of the UAE.

**Why It's Special:** This opulent palace showcases Arabian craftsmanship and features exhibitions on the country's rich heritage.

- **Location:** Al Ras Al Akhdar, Abu Dhabi
- **Cost:** AED 60 (approximately $16 USD) for adults
- **Best Time to Visit:** Late afternoon to enjoy the light show that illuminates the palace
- **Insider Tip:** Don't miss the "Palace in Motion" light show, held in the evening, which tells the story of the UAE’s journey.

## Culture

### Louvre Abu Dhabi

**What It Is:** A stunning art and civilization museum located on Saadiyat Island.

**Why It's Special:** The Louvre Abu Dhabi showcases artworks from around the world, bridging the gap between Eastern and Western art.

- **Location:** Saadiyat Island, Abu Dhabi
- **Cost:** AED 63 (approximately $17 USD) for adults
- **Best Time to Visit:** Weekdays to avoid the weekend rush
- **Insider Tip:** Allocate at least 3-4 hours to explore the museum fully, and don't miss the spectacular rain of light effect in the museum's dome.

### Heritage Village

**What It Is:** A reconstructed traditional village that offers a glimpse into the UAE's past.

**Why It's Special:** This living museum features everything from a souk and a mosque to workshops where artisans demonstrate traditional skills.

- **Location:** Near Marina Mall, Abu Dhabi
- **Cost:** Free entry
- **Best Time to Visit:** Mornings when demonstrations are more frequent
- **Insider Tip:** Visit the small museum within the village to see a collection of historical artifacts and photographs.

## Food

### Emirates Palace Afternoon Tea

**What It Is:** A luxurious afternoon tea experience at one of the world’s most opulent hotels.

**Why It's Special:** Known for its grandeur, the Emirates Palace offers a lavish setting for indulging in fine teas and pastries.

- **Location:** West Corniche Road, Abu Dhabi
- **Cost:** AED 310 (approximately $84 USD) per person
- **Best Time to Visit:** Afternoon to enjoy the full tea service
- **Insider Tip:** Reserve in advance, and while you’re there, explore the hotel’s stunning lobby and grounds.

### Al Maryah Island Dining

**What It Is:** A hub for high-end dining with numerous international cuisines.

**Why It's Special:** From waterfront dining to rooftop lounges, Al Maryah Island offers an array of culinary experiences.

- **Location:** Al Maryah Island, Abu Dhabi
- **Cost:** Varies by restaurant; expect to pay AED 150-500 per meal ($40-$136 USD)
- **Best Time to Visit:** Evening for dinner
- **Insider Tip:** Try Zuma for Japanese cuisine or Coya for Peruvian flavors, both offering exceptional views and service.

## Nature

### Mangrove National Park

**What It Is:** A protected area that offers a unique natural habitat for marine life and bird species.

**Why It's Special:** This ecological treasure allows visitors to explore mangrove forests by kayak or boat, offering a tranquil escape from the city.

- **Location:** Eastern Mangrove Lagoon National Park, Abu Dhabi
- **Cost:** Kayak rental from AED 80 (approximately $22 USD)
- **Best Time to Visit:** Early morning or late afternoon to avoid the midday heat
- **Insider Tip:** Book a guided kayak tour to learn more about the ecosystem and spot wildlife like flamingos and herons.

### Al Ain Oasis

**What It Is:** A UNESCO World Heritage site featuring a lush oasis in the desert city of Al Ain.

**Why It's Special:** The oasis covers 1,200 hectares and showcases traditional falaj irrigation systems, offering a green respite in the desert.

- **Location:** Al Ain, Abu Dhabi
- **Cost:** Free entry
- **Best Time to Visit:** Early morning or late afternoon for cooler weather
- **Insider Tip:** Use the shaded pathways for a more comfortable exploration, and visit the nearby Al Ain National Museum for historical context.

## Entertainment

### Ferrari World Abu Dhabi

**What It Is:** The world’s first Ferrari-themed park offering thrilling rides and attractions.

**Why It's Special:** Home to the world’s fastest roller coaster, Formula Rossa, this park is a dream destination for thrill-seekers.

- **Location:** Yas Island, Abu Dhabi
- **Cost:** AED 295 (approximately $80 USD) for adults
- **Best Time to Visit:** Weekdays to avoid long lines
- **Insider Tip:** Arrive early to beat the crowds and experience the most popular rides without long waits.

### Yas Waterworld

**What It Is:** An Emirati-themed water park with over 40 rides, slides, and attractions.

**Why It's Special:** Known for its adrenaline-pumping rides as well as more relaxed attractions, it's perfect for families and thrill-seekers alike.

- **Location:** Yas Island, Abu Dhabi
- **Cost:** AED 270 (approximately $73 USD) for adults
- **Best Time to Visit:** Weekdays and cooler months for a more pleasant experience
- **Insider Tip:** Bring sunscreen and water shoes, as the ground can get hot in the sun.

### Warner Bros. World Abu Dhabi

**What It Is:** An indoor theme park featuring Warner Bros. characters and animations.

**Why It's Special:** With six immersive lands and numerous rides, it’s one of the largest indoor theme parks in the world.

- **Location:** Yas Island, Abu Dhabi
- **Cost:** AED 295 (approximately $80 USD) for adults
- **Best Time to Visit:** Early in the day to maximize your time in the park
- **Insider Tip:** Download the park app to check ride wait times and show schedules.

## Shopping

### Yas Mall

**What It Is:** Abu Dhabi’s largest shopping mall, offering a mix of luxury and high-street brands.

**Why It's Special:** Besides shopping, Yas Mall offers a variety of dining options and entertainment venues, including a cinema.

- **Location:** Yas Island, Abu Dhabi
- **Cost:** Free entry; shopping costs vary
- **Best Time to Visit:** Weekdays to avoid crowds
- **Insider Tip:** Take advantage of the mall's concierge service for assistance with shopping and dining reservations.

### Souk Central Market

**What It Is:** A modern twist on a traditional souk, offering a vibrant shopping experience.

**Why It's Special:** Featuring a mix of traditional and contemporary shops, it’s a great place to buy unique souvenirs.

- **Location:** Zayed the First Street, Abu Dhabi
- **Cost:** Free entry; prices vary by vendor
- **Best Time to Visit:** Late afternoon or evening for a lively atmosphere
- **Insider Tip:** Don’t be afraid to haggle for a better price, especially in the traditional stalls.

## Conclusion

Abu Dhabi offers a rich array of experiences that cater to every type of traveler. From exploring the grandeur of the Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque to thrilling rides at Ferrari World, the city is a blend of cultural richness and modern excitement. Whether you're dining at a luxurious hotel, exploring natural wonders, or shopping in vibrant markets, Abu Dhabi promises an unforgettable journey. Plan your itinerary well, and you'll discover why this city is a jewel of the UAE.
