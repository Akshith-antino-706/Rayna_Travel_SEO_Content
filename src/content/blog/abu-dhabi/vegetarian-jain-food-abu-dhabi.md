# Vegetarian and Vegan Food Guide to Abu Dhabi

Abu Dhabi, the capital of the United Arab Emirates, is a bustling metropolis known for its luxurious lifestyle, stunning architecture, and diverse culinary scene. While Middle Eastern cuisine typically features a variety of meat dishes, Abu Dhabi has become increasingly friendly to vegetarians and vegans. Whether you're a local, an expat, or a tourist, you'll find plenty of options to satisfy your plant-based cravings. This guide will help you navigate the city's offerings, from traditional vegetarian dishes to modern vegan eateries.

## Vegetarian and Vegan-Friendly Atmosphere

### How Vegetarian-Friendly is Abu Dhabi?

Abu Dhabi is progressively embracing vegetarianism and veganism, reflecting global trends and the growing demand for plant-based diets. While traditional Emirati cuisine often includes meat, the city's international dining scene caters to a variety of dietary preferences. Many restaurants offer vegetarian options, and dedicated vegetarian and vegan establishments are becoming more common.

**Tip:** While dining out, don't hesitate to ask for vegetarian modifications. Most chefs are accommodating and willing to adjust their dishes.

### Local Vegetarian Dishes

- **Falafel:** Deep-fried balls made from ground chickpeas, often served in a pita with vegetables and tahini sauce.
- **Hummus:** A creamy dip made from blended chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, and garlic.
- **Tabbouleh:** A fresh salad made with parsley, tomatoes, mint, onion, and bulgur, dressed with olive oil and lemon juice.
- **Fattoush:** A salad featuring mixed greens, tomatoes, radishes, and crispy pita croutons.
- **Manakish Zaatar:** A type of flatbread topped with a mixture of thyme, sumac, and sesame seeds.

## Jain Food Availability

Jain food, which excludes root vegetables and follows strict dietary laws, can be more challenging to find. However, Indian restaurants are your best bet for Jain-compatible meals.

### Jain-Friendly Restaurants

- **Address:** Hamdan Street, Abu Dhabi
- **Price Range:** 20-50 AED ($5-$14)
- **Highlight:** Offers a dedicated Jain menu with dishes like Jain thali and Jain dosas.

### 10. **Saravanaa Bhavan**
- **Address:** Airport Road, Abu Dhabi
- **Price Range:** 20-60 AED ($5-$16)
- **Highlight:** This popular chain offers a wide range of Indian vegetarian dishes, with Jain options available upon request.

## Communicating Dietary Needs

### Useful Phrases in Arabic

- **Vegetarian:** نباتي (Nabati)
- **Vegan:** نباتي صرف (Nabati Sarf)
- **No meat, please:** بدون لحم، من فضلك (Bedoon lahem, min fadlak)
- **No dairy, please:** بدون منتجات الألبان، من فضلك (Bedoon montajat al-alban, min fadlak)
- **I am allergic to...:** لدي حساسية تجاه... (Laday hasasiya tijah...)

**Insider Tip:** Learning a few key phrases can be helpful in communicating your dietary preferences, especially in local markets and smaller eateries.

## Vegetarian Street Food

While street food in Abu Dhabi is often meat-focused, you can still find delicious vegetarian snacks.

### Popular Vegetarian Street Foods

- **Samosas:** Triangular pastries filled with spiced potatoes and peas.
- **Karak Chai:** A spiced tea made with milk and cardamom, perfect for a mid-day break.
- **Baklava:** A sweet pastry made with layers of filo dough, nuts, and honey.
