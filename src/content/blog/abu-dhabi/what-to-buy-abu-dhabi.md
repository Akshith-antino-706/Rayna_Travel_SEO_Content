# Souvenir and Shopping Guide for Tourists in Abu Dhabi

Abu Dhabi, the capital of the United Arab Emirates, is a treasure trove of unique shopping experiences and souvenirs that capture the essence of Emirati culture and lifestyle. From traditional crafts and local specialties to modern art and luxury goods, there is something for every traveler. This guide explores the best items to bring home, where to find them, and tips for a seamless shopping experience.

## Traditional Crafts and Local Specialties

### 1. Aladdin Lamps
**Where to Buy:** Souk Al Zafarana, located in Al Ain, about a 1.5-hour drive from Abu Dhabi city center.  
**Price Range:** AED 50-150 (USD 14-41) depending on size and detail.  
**Authenticity Tips:** Look for lamps made from brass with intricate designs. Avoid plastic replicas often found in tourist areas.  
**Customs Considerations:** Generally easy to transport, but check with your airline for specific regulations on metal items.

### 2. Arabian Coffee Pots (Dallah)
**Where to Buy:** The Iranian Souk in Mina Zayed.  
**Price Range:** AED 100-300 (USD 27-82).  
**Authenticity Tips:** Genuine dallahs are made from brass or copper and may have intricate engravings.  
**Insider Tip:** Visit in the morning to avoid the afternoon heat and crowds.

### 3. Handwoven Carpets
**Where to Buy:** Carpet Souk, Mina Port.  
**Price Range:** AED 500-5,000 (USD 136-1,360) depending on size and quality.  
**Authenticity Tips:** Ask for a certificate of authenticity for higher-end purchases.  
**Customs Considerations:** Be aware of the import regulations for textiles in your country.

## Food and Spices

### 4. Saffron
**Where to Buy:** Al Mina Fruit & Vegetable Souk.  
**Price Range:** AED 30-70 (USD 8-19) per gram.  
**Authenticity Tips:** The best saffron has a deep red color and a strong aroma.  
**Tip:** Store saffron in a cool, dark place to maintain its quality.

### 5. Dates
**Where to Buy:** Bateel stores located throughout Abu Dhabi, including The Galleria on Al Maryah Island.  
**Price Range:** AED 30-150 (USD 8-41) per box.  
**Authenticity Tips:** Bateel is known for premium quality dates, offering a variety of stuffed and plain dates.  
**Customs Considerations:** Most countries allow the import of packaged dates.

### 6. Arabic Sweets
**Where to Buy:** Al Baba Sweets, located in Zayed The First St, Al Khalidiyah.  
**Price Range:** AED 50-200 (USD 14-55) per box.  
**Authenticity Tips:** Look for sweets made with natural ingredients and check the production date for freshness.

## Clothing and Textiles

### 7. Abayas
**Where to Buy:** Al Wahda Mall or Yas Mall.  
**Price Range:** AED 200-1,000 (USD 55-272).  
**Authenticity Tips:** Choose abayas made from high-quality fabrics like silk or cotton.  
**Insider Tip:** Many stores offer customization to add a personal touch.

### 8. Kanduras
**Where to Buy:** The Souk at Qaryat Al Beri.  
**Price Range:** AED 150-800 (USD 41-218).  
**Authenticity Tips:** Traditional kanduras are made of breathable cotton to suit the desert climate.

## Art and Jewelry

### 9. Pearl Jewelry
**Where to Buy:** Amouage, located in Marina Mall.  
**Price Range:** AED 500-5,000 (USD 136-1,360).  
**Authenticity Tips:** Ensure pearls are natural, and ask for authenticity certificates.  
**Customs Considerations:** Check import duties for jewelry in your home country.

### 10. Arabic Calligraphy Art
**Where to Buy:** Etihad Modern Art Gallery, Al Bateen.  
**Price Range:** AED 200-2,000 (USD 55-545).  
**Authenticity Tips:** Look for works by local artists, often signed.

## Unique Gifts

### 11. Oud (Fragrance)
**Where to Buy:** Ajmal Perfumes, Abu Dhabi Mall.  
**Price Range:** AED 150-1,500 (USD 41-408).  
**Authenticity Tips:** Pure oud has a rich, woody aroma and is often sold by weight.

### 12. Camel Milk Chocolate
**Where to Buy:** Al Nassma in Yas Mall.  
**Price Range:** AED 35-100 (USD 9-27) per box.  
**Authenticity Tips:** Al Nassma is the first and only company producing camel milk chocolate.

## What NOT to Buy

### 13. Overpriced Replica Artifacts
**Why to Avoid:** Often sold at inflated prices in tourist-heavy areas like the Corniche.  
**Tip:** Genuine artifacts require provenance and are better purchased at reputable galleries or museums.

### 14. Mass-produced Souvenirs
**Why to Avoid:** Items like keychains and magnets can be found anywhere and are often not reflective of local craftsmanship.

## Best Shopping Areas

### The Galleria Al Maryah Island
A luxury shopping destination with high-end brands and fine dining options.

### Yas Mall
Offers a wide range of stores, from high street to luxury, and is conveniently located near Yas Island attractions.

### Souk Central Market
A modern twist on the traditional market, offering a variety of local and international goods.

## Conclusion

Shopping in Abu Dhabi offers a blend of traditional and modern experiences, with opportunities to purchase authentic, high-quality products that reflect the rich culture of the UAE. By knowing where to shop and what to look for, you can bring home meaningful souvenirs that capture the spirit of Abu Dhabi. Always remember to check authenticity, avoid overpriced tourist traps, and consider customs regulations when purchasing your treasures. Enjoy your shopping journey in the heart of the UAE!
