# A Street Food Lover's Guide to Dubai

Dubai is a vibrant city known for its luxurious lifestyle, but its street food scene is equally impressive. From aromatic spices to sweet treats, the street food culture in Dubai is a melting pot of flavors reflecting its diverse heritage. This guide explores 15 iconic street foods worth trying, along with where to find them, estimated costs, and tips for a safe and enjoyable experience.

## Iconic Street Foods in Dubai

### 1. Shawarma
**Description:** A Middle Eastern classic, shawarma consists of marinated meat (usually chicken, beef, or lamb) cooked on a rotating spit and served in flatbread with garlic sauce and pickles.  
**Where to Find:** Al Mallah in Satwa is famous for its shawarma.  
**Estimated Cost:** AED 10-15.  
**Best Time to Visit:** Late night, around 10 PM for the best crowd.  
**Hygiene Information:** Look for stalls with a steady flow of customers for freshness.

### 2. Falafel
**Description:** Deep-fried balls made from ground chickpeas, herbs, and spices, falafel is a popular vegetarian option served in pita with tahini sauce.  
**Where to Find:** Try the falafel at the Ripe Market in Al Ain, or head to the street vendors in Jumeirah.  
**Estimated Cost:** AED 5-10.  
**Best Time to Visit:** Lunchtime, around 1 PM.  
**Hygiene Information:** Opt for places that prepare the falafel fresh on-site.

### 3. Manakish
**Description:** A traditional Lebanese flatbread topped with za'atar, cheese, or minced meat, manakish is often enjoyed as a breakfast item.  
**Where to Find:** Zaatar w Zeit in multiple locations, or the local bakeries in Karama.  
**Estimated Cost:** AED 10-20.  
**Best Time to Visit:** Breakfast hours, from 7 AM to 10 AM.  
**Hygiene Information:** Choose vendors that display freshly made products.

### 4. Samosa
**Description:** These crispy pastries filled with spiced potatoes or meat are a favorite snack, especially during Ramadan.  
**Where to Find:** Head to the Old Souk in Deira for authentic samosas from local vendors.  
**Estimated Cost:** AED 2-5 per piece.  
**Best Time to Visit:** Late afternoon or early evening, around 5 PM.  
**Hygiene Information:** Ensure the vendor maintains cleanliness in both food preparation and display.

### 5. Kebab
**Description:** Grilled skewers of marinated meat (beef, chicken, or lamb) seasoned with spices, typically served with pita bread.  
**Where to Find:** Al Dhiyafah Road in Jumeirah is home to several kebab stalls.  
**Estimated Cost:** AED 15-30.  
**Best Time to Visit:** Evening, around 8 PM.  
**Hygiene Information:** Look for vendors with a visible grill and a busy setup.

### 6. Hummus with Pita
**Description:** A creamy dip made from blended chickpeas, tahini, and olive oil, accompanied by warm pita bread.  
**Where to Find:** Most Arabic restaurants and food stalls, particularly in Al Quoz.  
**Estimated Cost:** AED 10-15.  
**Best Time to Visit:** Afternoon, around 3 PM.  
**Hygiene Information:** Choose places that serve fresh hummus daily.

### 7. Kunafa
**Description:** A dessert made of shredded phyllo pastry soaked in syrup and filled with cheese or cream.  
**Where to Find:** Al Kebabji in Al Jafiliya is well-known for its kunafa.  
**Estimated Cost:** AED 15-25.  
**Best Time to Visit:** Evening, around 9 PM.  
**Hygiene Information:** Inspect the cleanliness of the dessert display.

### 8. Chaat
**Description:** A spicy Indian snack mix that includes crispy fried dough, chickpeas, potatoes, and tangy chutneys.  
**Where to Find:** The streets of Bur Dubai and Karama.  
**Estimated Cost:** AED 10-15.  
**Best Time to Visit:** Late afternoon, around 4 PM.  
**Hygiene Information:** Opt for well-frequented stalls for quality assurance.

### 9. Biryani
**Description:** Fragrant rice dish mixed with marinated meat, spices, and often topped with fried onions and boiled eggs.  
**Where to Find:** The Al Fahidi area has several food stalls offering biryani.  
**Estimated Cost:** AED 15-35.  
**Best Time to Visit:** Lunchtime, around 1 PM.  
**Hygiene Information:** Look for vendors with a good reputation and positive reviews.

### 10. Fresh Juices
**Description:** Dubai's street vendors serve a variety of fresh juices made from fruits like mango, pomegranate, and watermelon.  
**Where to Find:** The Global Village and Souk Madinat Jumeirah are great spots.  
**Estimated Cost:** AED 10-15.  
**Best Time to Visit:** Hot afternoons, especially from 2 PM to 5 PM.  
**Hygiene Information:** Choose vendors that prepare juices in front of you.

### 11. Mandi
**Description:** A spiced rice dish with meat, traditionally cooked in a tandoor; it's a staple in Emirati cuisine.  
**Where to Find:** Al Ain and the Mandi stalls in Al Quoz.  
**Estimated Cost:** AED 20-50.  
**Best Time to Visit:** Dinner time, around 7 PM.  
**Hygiene Information:** Pick busy places where the food is freshly made.

### 12. Pani Puri
**Description:** Hollow, crispy puris filled with spiced water, chickpeas, and potatoes; a popular Indian street food.  
**Where to Find:** Various vendors around Bur Dubai.  
**Estimated Cost:** AED 10-15.  
**Best Time to Visit:** Late afternoon or evening.  
**Hygiene Information:** Ensure the water used is filtered or bottled.

### 13. Cheese Roll
**Description:** A fried roll filled with cheese and spices, often enjoyed as a snack.  
**Where to Find:** Local bakeries in Deira and Karama.  
**Estimated Cost:** AED 5-10.  
**Best Time to Visit:** Anytime during the day.  
**Hygiene Information:** Inspect the freshness of the fried items.

### 14. Tandoori Chicken
**Description:** Marinated chicken cooked in a clay oven and served with mint chutney and naan.  
**Where to Find:** Tandoor Chops in Jumeirah serves a great version.  
**Estimated Cost:** AED 15-30.  
**Best Time to Visit:** Evening, around 6 PM.  
**Hygiene Information:** Look for smoke and a clean cooking area.
