# Family Activities Guide to Dubai, United Arab Emirates

Dubai, a city known for its futuristic skyline, luxurious shopping malls, and vibrant culture, is also a paradise for families seeking adventure and fun. From thrilling theme parks to educational museums, Dubai offers a plethora of family-friendly activities. Here’s your ultimate guide to exploring Dubai with children.

## 1. Theme Parks

### IMG Worlds of Adventure
**Age Suitability:** 5 years and above  
**Cost:** AED 335 per person (approximately $91)  
**Facilities:** Stroller rentals, baby changing rooms, dining options  
**Opening Hours:** 12:00 PM – 10:00 PM (Sunday to Wednesday), 12:00 PM – 11:00 PM (Thursday to Saturday)  

IMG Worlds of Adventure is the largest indoor theme park in Dubai, offering various zones themed around popular cartoon characters and superheroes. With adrenaline-pumping rides and immersive experiences, it’s a hit with older children and teenagers.

**Tip:** Purchase tickets online in advance to avoid long queues and enjoy discounts.

### Legoland Dubai
**Age Suitability:** 2 to 12 years  
**Cost:** AED 295 per person (approximately $80)  
**Facilities:** Stroller rentals, baby care centers, dining options with kids' menus  
**Opening Hours:** 10:00 AM – 6:00 PM (varies seasonally)  

Located in Dubai Parks and Resorts, Legoland is perfect for younger kids, with over 40 interactive rides, building experiences, and attractions. The park is divided into different themed areas, including Lego City and Adventure.

**Insider Tip:** Visit the park early to avoid the afternoon heat and crowds.

## 2. Zoos and Aquariums

### Dubai Aquarium & Underwater Zoo
**Age Suitability:** All ages  
**Cost:** AED 120 per person (approximately $33)  
**Facilities:** Stroller access, feeding areas, cafes  
**Opening Hours:** 10:00 AM – 12:00 AM (midnight)  

Located in The Dubai Mall, this aquarium is home to thousands of aquatic animals and offers a walk-through experience with a 48-meter tunnel. The underwater zoo features unique species such as King Croc and playful penguins.

**Tip:** Combine your visit with a trip to the mall’s other attractions for a full day of entertainment.

### The Green Planet
**Age Suitability:** 3 years and above  
**Cost:** AED 110 per person (approximately $30)  
**Facilities:** Stroller-friendly paths, restrooms, cafes  
**Opening Hours:** 10:00 AM – 6:00 PM  

This indoor rainforest in City Walk Dubai allows families to explore over 3,000 plants and animals. It’s an educational experience that encourages children to learn about biodiversity and conservation.

**Insider Tip:** Attend the interactive animal encounters for a more hands-on experience.

## 3. Interactive Museums

### OliOli Children's Museum
**Age Suitability:** 1 to 12 years  
**Cost:** AED 126 per child (approximately $34), AED 42 per adult (approximately $11)  
**Facilities:** Baby changing rooms, stroller access, snack bars  
**Opening Hours:** 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM  

OliOli is an interactive museum designed to stimulate a child’s imagination through play. With themed galleries such as Air, Water, and Future Park, children can engage in hands-on activities and creative learning experiences.

**Tip:** Check the museum’s schedule for special workshops and events.

### Museum of Illusions
**Age Suitability:** 5 years and above  
**Cost:** AED 80 per adult (approximately $22), AED 60 per child (approximately $16)  
**Facilities:** Restrooms, gift shop  
**Opening Hours:** 10:00 AM – 10:00 PM  

This museum offers mind-bending optical illusions and interactive exhibits that are both fun and educational. It’s a great place for families to explore and take memorable photos.

**Insider Tip:** Visit during weekdays to avoid crowds and enjoy a more intimate experience.

## 4. Parks and Outdoor Activities

### Dubai Miracle Garden
**Age Suitability:** All ages  
**Cost:** AED 55 per adult (approximately $15), AED 40 per child (approximately $11)  
**Facilities:** Wheelchair and stroller access, food kiosks, restrooms  
**Opening Hours:** 9:00 AM – 9:00 PM (Sunday to Thursday), 9:00 AM – 11:00 PM (Friday and Saturday)  

This floral wonderland features stunning displays of vibrant blooms and themed gardens. It’s a delightful spot for families to enjoy a leisurely stroll among millions of flowers.

**Tip:** Visit during the cooler months (November to March) for the best experience.

### Jumeirah Beach Park
**Age Suitability:** All ages  
**Cost:** AED 5 per person (approximately $1.36)  
**Facilities:** Picnic areas, children’s play zones, restrooms  
**Opening Hours:** 7:30 AM – 10:00 PM  

A popular spot for family picnics and beach activities, Jumeirah Beach Park offers clean sandy shores and clear waters. Facilities include shaded areas and play equipment for kids.

**Insider Tip:** Bring sunscreen and plenty of water, especially during the hotter months.

## 5. Nature and Adventure

### Dubai Desert Safari
**Age Suitability:** 6 years and above  
**Cost:** AED 150 per person (approximately $41)  
**Facilities:** Rest stops with restrooms, refreshments available  
**Timing:** Various tours available, typically in the afternoon  

Experience the thrill of dune bashing, camel rides, and a traditional Bedouin camp dinner in the heart of the desert. This adventure is perfect for families with older children who can enjoy the bumpy ride.

**Tip:** Wear comfortable clothing and bring a light jacket for the evening chill.

### Al Marmoom Bedouin Experience
**Age Suitability:** All ages  
**Cost:** AED 375 per adult (approximately $102), AED 250 per child (approximately $68)  
**Facilities:** Restrooms, dining facilities  
**Timing:** Evening tours  

This cultural experience offers a glimpse into the traditional Bedouin lifestyle with activities such as falconry, henna painting, and a traditional Emirati meal under the stars.

**Insider Tip:** Book in advance as these experiences often sell out quickly.

## 6. Kid-Friendly Restaurants

### Rainforest Cafe
**Age Suitability:** All ages  
**Location:** The Dubai Mall  
**Cost:** Average AED 150 per meal (approximately $41)  
**Facilities:** High chairs, children’s menu, themed decor  

An exciting dining spot for kids, the Rainforest Cafe offers a jungle-themed environment with animatronic animals and thunderstorms. The menu includes a wide variety of dishes suitable for all ages.

**Tip:** Reserve a table to ensure seating, especially during peak hours.

### The Cheesecake Factory
**Age Suitability:** All ages  
**Location:** Multiple locations including The Dubai Mall and Mall of the Emirates  
**Cost:** Average AED 120 per meal (approximately $33)  
**Facilities:** High chairs, children’s menu  

Known for its extensive menu and delicious cheesecakes, this restaurant caters to families with various dietary preferences.

**Insider Tip:** Portions are large, so consider sharing meals to avoid wastage.

## 7. Educational Experiences

### KidZania Dubai
**Age Suitability:** 4 to 16 years  
**Cost:** AED 180 per child (approximately $49), AED 75 per adult (approximately $20)  
**Facilities:** Stroller parking, baby changing rooms, cafes  
**Opening Hours:** 10:00 AM – 10:00 PM  

Located in The Dubai Mall, KidZania is an interactive city where kids can role-play over 80 professions. It’s an educational and entertaining experience that encourages learning through play.

**Tip:** Plan to spend at least 4-5 hours to fully enjoy the activities.

### Dubai Dolphinarium
**Age Suitability:** All ages  
**Cost:** AED 105 per adult (approximately $29), AED 50 per child (approximately $14)  
**Facilities:** Stroller access, restrooms, snack bars  
**Show Times:** 11:00 AM, 2:00 PM, 6:00 PM  

Witness the incredible performances of dolphins and seals at this indoor facility located in Creek Park. The shows are both entertaining and educational, making it a great family outing.

**Insider Tip:** Arrive early for good seats and consider adding a swim with dolphins experience.

## General Family Travel Tips for Dubai

- **Weather Considerations:** Dubai can be extremely hot, especially from June to September. Plan outdoor activities for early mornings or late afternoons and keep hydrated.
- **Dress Code:** While Dubai is modern, it’s respectful to dress modestly in public areas. Lightweight fabrics are recommended.
- **Transportation:** The Dubai Metro is a convenient and affordable way to travel with kids. Taxis are also readily available and reasonably priced.
- **Safety:** Dubai is one of the safest cities in the world, but always keep an eye on your belongings and children, especially in crowded areas.
- **Cultural Sensitivity:** Be mindful of local customs and traditions, particularly during the holy month of Ramadan when eating and drinking in public during daylight hours is restricted.

Dubai offers an endless array of activities that cater to every family’s interests. Whether you’re seeking adventure, education, or relaxation, this city promises an unforgettable family vacation. Enjoy your time in Dubai and make the most of these incredible experiences!
