# First-Time Visitor's Guide to Singapore

Singapore, a vibrant city-state in Southeast Asia, is known for its harmonious blend of cultures, stunning architecture, and mouthwatering food. Whether you're interested in exploring lush gardens, historical sites, or indulging in local cuisine, this guide will help you navigate your first visit to this dynamic city. 

## Pre-Trip Planning

### Getting There
Singapore's main gateway is **Changi Airport (SIN)**, consistently rated as one of the best airports in the world. Major airlines such as Singapore Airlines, Emirates, and Qantas operate flights to Singapore, making it accessible from almost anywhere. Depending on your location, round-trip fares can range from $500 to $1,500, so it's wise to book in advance.

### Best Times to Visit
The best time to visit Singapore is from **February to April**. During these months, the weather is relatively cooler and less humid. While Singapore has a tropical climate and can be visited year-round, be prepared for sudden rain showers, especially during the monsoon season from November to January.

### Visa Requirements
Most tourists can enter Singapore without a visa for up to 30 days. However, always check the latest regulations based on your nationality. Ensure your passport is valid for at least six months beyond your planned departure date.

## Arrival

### Customs and Immigration
Upon landing, go through customs and immigration. The process is typically efficient, but be prepared to show your return ticket and proof of accommodation. 

### Currency and Budgeting
The local currency is the **Singapore Dollar (SGD)**. As of late 2023, the exchange rate is approximately **1 USD to 1.35 SGD**. A daily budget of **$50 to $100** per person is reasonable, depending on your travel style. 

### SIM Cards and Wi-Fi
Purchasing a local SIM card at the airport or convenience stores is a practical choice for staying connected. Look for options like **Singtel**, **StarHub**, or **M1**, where plans typically start from **$10 SGD** for data packages. Free Wi-Fi is also available in many public places and malls.

## Getting Around

Singapore boasts an impressive and efficient public transport system. 

### Public Transportation
- **MRT (Mass Rapid Transit)**: This is the backbone of public transport. Fares range from **$0.80 to $2.00** depending on distance. Purchase an **EZ-Link card** for convenient travel.
- **Buses**: Complement the MRT and are an affordable option. Fares are similar to the MRT, and transfers are easy.
- **Taxis**: While more expensive (starting at around **$3 SGD**), taxis are a convenient option for late-night travel.

### Walking and Biking
Many attractions are within walking distance of each other. Consider renting a bike through the **SG Bike** or **Anywheel** services for a fun and eco-friendly way to explore.

## What to See

### Major Attractions
- **Marina Bay Sands**: Iconic skyline, infinity pool, and the SkyPark observation deck offer breathtaking views. Entry to the SkyPark costs around **$20 SGD**.
- **Gardens by the Bay**: Explore the futuristic Supertree Grove and the stunning Flower Dome. Admission is approximately **$28 SGD** for adults.
- **Chinatown**: Rich in history, with temples like the **Buddha Tooth Relic Temple**. Enjoy local street food and shop for souvenirs.
- **Little India**: A vibrant neighborhood showcasing Indian culture, complete with colorful temples and the famous **Tekka Centre** for local food.
- **Orchard Road**: A shopping paradise with luxury and high-street brands.

### Cultural Highlights
- **National Gallery Singapore**: Houses a vast collection of Southeast Asian art. Admission is around **$20 SGD**.
- **Kampong Glam**: The Malay heritage district featuring the stunning **Sultan Mosque** and hip cafes.

## Where to Eat

Singapore is a food lover's paradise, offering a spectrum of dining options.

### Hawker Centres
- **Maxwell Food Centre**: Famous for its Hainanese chicken rice (try **Tian Tian Chicken Rice**).
- **Lau Pa Sat**: Iconic for its satay stalls in the evening.
- **Chomp Chomp Food Centre**: Known for local delights like BBQ stingray and fried carrot cake.

### Mid-Range Restaurants
- **Chatterbox**: Located in Hilton Singapore, offers award-winning Mandarin chicken rice.
- **The Coconut Club**: Known for its Nasi Lemak, this spot is a local favorite.

### Fine Dining
- **Odette**: A Michelin-starred restaurant offering modern French cuisine.
- **CE LA VI**: Located on Marina Bay Sands, this rooftop restaurant combines stunning views with an exquisite menu.

## Practical Information

### Safety Tips
Singapore is one of the safest cities in the world. However, follow these tips:
- Stay vigilant in crowded areas.
- Avoid walking alone in poorly lit areas at night.
- Always adhere to local laws, including strict regulations against littering and jaywalking.

### Language
The official languages are English, Mandarin, Malay, and Tamil. English is widely spoken, making communication easy for tourists.

### Customs and Etiquette
Singapore is a multicultural society; respect local customs and traditions. For example, remove your shoes when entering a home or temple.

- Police: **999**
- Ambulance and Fire: **995**
