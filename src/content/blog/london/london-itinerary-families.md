# A Family-Friendly London Itinerary: Exploring the Best of London with Kids

London is a vibrant city filled with attractions that cater to families and children of all ages. This itinerary is designed to help families explore the best of London while ensuring that kids stay engaged, happy, and well-rested. 

## Day 1: Exploring Central London

### Morning: Visit the Natural History Museum
**Time:** 9:30 AM - 11:30 AM  
**Location:** Cromwell Road, South Kensington, SW7 5BD  
**Cost:** Free (donations welcome)  
**Restrooms:** Available throughout the museum  
**Accessibility:** Wheelchair accessible  

Kick off your London adventure at the Natural History Museum, where children can marvel at dinosaur skeletons and learn about the natural world through interactive exhibits. 

**Tip:** Arrive early to avoid the crowds and head straight to the dinosaur gallery. 

### Mid-Morning Snack Break
**Time:** 11:30 AM - 12:00 PM  
**Restaurant:** The Natural History Museum Café  
**Menu Highlights:** Sandwiches, pastries, and kid-friendly meals. Prices range from £4 to £10.

### Afternoon: Explore Hyde Park
**Time:** 12:00 PM - 2:00 PM  
**Location:** Kensington Gardens, London W2 2UH  
**Activity:** Picnic and Playground  
**Cost:** Free  

After your museum visit, take a leisurely stroll to Hyde Park, where kids can burn off energy at the Diana Memorial Playground. Pack a picnic or grab lunch from the café nearby. 

**Tip:** Bring a blanket and enjoy lunch under the shade of a tree.

### Rest Time
**Time:** 2:00 PM - 3:00 PM  
**Location:** Find a quiet spot in Hyde Park for a nap or quiet time.  

### Late Afternoon: Visit the Science Museum
**Time:** 3:00 PM - 5:00 PM  
**Location:** Exhibition Road, South Kensington, SW7 2DD  
**Cost:** Free (donations welcome)  
**Restrooms:** Available throughout the museum  
**Accessibility:** Wheelchair accessible  

Just a short walk from Hyde Park, the Science Museum features hands-on exhibits perfect for children. The Wonderlab, in particular, is a hit with kids of all ages.

### Dinner
**Time:** 5:30 PM - 7:00 PM  
**Restaurant:** Dishoom (Indian cuisine)  
**Location:** 12 Upper St. Martin's Lane, London WC2H 9FB  
**Menu Highlights:** Family-style dining with dishes starting around £6.  
**Tip:** Arrive early or book a table in advance.

## Day 2: A Day of Adventure at Theme Parks

### Morning: Legoland Windsor
**Time:** 9:00 AM - 12:30 PM  
**Location:** Winkfield Rd, Windsor SL4 4AY  
**Cost:** Tickets from £39 online  
**Accessibility:** Wheelchair accessible  

Take a short train ride from London to Windsor to experience Legoland. This theme park is perfect for younger kids, featuring rides, shows, and Lego-building workshops.

### Lunch
**Time:** 12:30 PM - 1:30 PM  
**Restaurant:** The Pizza Pasta Buffet at Legoland  
**Menu Highlights:** Pizza, pasta, and salads, with prices around £12 for adults and £8 for children.

### Afternoon: Continue at Legoland
**Time:** 1:30 PM - 4:00 PM  
**Activity:** Explore more rides and attractions.  

### Rest Time
**Time:** 4:00 PM - 5:00 PM  
**Location:** Take a break at one of the shaded picnic areas within the park.

### Dinner
**Time:** 5:30 PM - 7:00 PM  
**Restaurant:** The Royal Oak (family-friendly pub)  
**Location:** 4 The Green, Windsor SL4 6NQ  
**Menu Highlights:** Traditional British fare with kids' meals around £6-8. 

## Day 3: Museums and Markets

### Morning: The London Zoo
**Time:** 9:00 AM - 12:00 PM  
**Location:** Outer Circle, Regent's Park, NW1 4RY  
**Cost:** Tickets from £27 online  
**Restrooms:** Available throughout the zoo  
**Accessibility:** Wheelchair accessible  

Start your day at the London Zoo, where children can see animals up close and enjoy interactive exhibits.

### Lunch
**Time:** 12:00 PM - 1:00 PM  
**Restaurant:** The Terrace Restaurant at the Zoo  
**Menu Highlights:** Family-friendly meals starting around £10. 

### Afternoon: Visit the British Museum
**Time:** 1:30 PM - 3:30 PM  
**Location:** Great Russell St, Bloomsbury, WC1B 3DG  
**Cost:** Free (donations welcome)  
**Restrooms:** Available throughout the museum  
**Accessibility:** Wheelchair accessible  

The British Museum is a treasure trove of history and a great place for children to learn about different cultures. Check out the interactive family trails available at the entrance.

### Late Afternoon: Explore Camden Market
**Time:** 4:00 PM - 6:00 PM  
**Location:** Camden Lock Place, NW1 8AF  

Stroll through Camden Market, known for its eclectic food stalls and shops. Kids can find unique souvenirs and enjoy street food from around the world.

### Dinner
**Time:** 6:00 PM - 7:30 PM  
**Restaurant:** The Cheese Bar (family-friendly option)  
**Menu Highlights:** Cheese dishes and grilled cheese sandwiches starting at £6.

## Transportation Tips with Kids
1. **Oyster Card or Contactless Payment:** Use an Oyster card or contactless payment to travel on public transport. Kids under 11 travel free on buses and the Tube with a paying adult.
2. **Stroller Friendly:** Most London Underground stations are equipped with lifts, making them stroller-friendly.
3. **Plan Your Routes:** Use apps like Citymapper to find the best routes and avoid crowded times.

## Kid-Friendly Entertainment Tips
- **Interactive Exhibits:** Look for hands-on exhibits in museums that engage children actively.
- **Rest Breaks:** Schedule downtime regularly to prevent overstimulation.
- **Snacks:** Always have healthy snacks on hand to keep energy levels up.

## Conclusion
With this family-friendly itinerary, you can explore the wonders of London while ensuring that everyone, from toddlers to teenagers, has an exciting and enjoyable experience. By incorporating interactive museums, theme parks, and restful breaks, you can create lasting memories in this vibrant city. Enjoy your adventure in London!
