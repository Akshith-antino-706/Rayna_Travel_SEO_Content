# Essential Emergency Contact Numbers for London

When traveling to London, it’s crucial to be prepared for any emergencies that might arise. This guide provides you with essential emergency contact numbers, embassy and consulate information, major hospitals, and non-emergency useful numbers to ensure your safety and peace of mind during your visit.

## Emergency Services

### Police, Ambulance, and Fire Department

In the event of an emergency, you can reach all emergency services in London by dialing **999**. This number connects you to the police, ambulance, and fire department.

- **Police**
  - **Emergency Number:** 999
  - **Non-Emergency Number:** 101
  - **What to Say:** Clearly state your emergency, provide your location, and describe the situation.

- **Ambulance**
  - **Emergency Number:** 999
  - **What to Say:** Provide your location and symptoms or the nature of the medical emergency.

- **Fire Department**
  - **Emergency Number:** 999
  - **What to Say:** State that it’s a fire emergency and give your location.

### Poison Control

- **National Poisons Information Service**
  - **Phone Number:** 111 (This number can also help with non-emergency medical advice)
  - **What to Say:** Provide details about the substance ingested and any symptoms.

### Tourist Police

- **Tourist Police (Metropolitan Police Service)**
  - **Phone Number:** 020 7230 1212
  - **What to Say:** If you feel unsafe or have been a victim of crime, explain your situation clearly.

## Calling Information

### Area Codes
- **Emergency Services:** Dial **999** for emergencies.
- **London Area Code:** 020 (for local calls within London)
- **International Dialing:** To call the UK from abroad, dial your country’s international access code (e.g., 011 from the USA) followed by the UK country code **44** and then the local number without the leading zero.

### Language
- **English is the primary language spoken in London**, and most emergency services are equipped to handle calls in English. 

### What to Say
When calling any emergency service or hospital:
1. **State the emergency clearly.**
2. **Provide your location.**
3. **Describe the situation briefly.**
4. **Stay calm and answer any questions asked.**
