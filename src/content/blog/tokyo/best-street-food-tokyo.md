# A Street Food Lover's Guide to Tokyo

Tokyo is a bustling metropolis renowned not only for its towering skyscrapers and rich history but also for its vibrant street food scene. From early morning breakfast bites to late-night snacks, Tokyo offers a delightful array of street foods that reflect the city's culinary diversity. Here’s a comprehensive guide to some of the most iconic street foods you can savor in Tokyo, including where to find them, what they cost, and essential tips for enjoying your culinary adventure.

## Morning Breakfast Specialties

### 1. Onigiri (Rice Balls)
- **Description**: Onigiri are triangular rice balls often filled with ingredients like pickled plum (umeboshi), grilled salmon, or seasoned seaweed.
- **Ingredients**: Rice, nori (seaweed), fillings (like tuna or pickled vegetables).
- **Where to Find**: Convenience stores like 7-Eleven, FamilyMart, or specialty shops like Onigiri Bongo in Shibuya.
- **Cost**: ¥100-¥300 ($1-$3).
- **Best Times to Visit**: Breakfast hours (7 AM - 10 AM).
- **Hygiene/Safety**: Very safe; look for refrigerated options.

### 2. Tamagoyaki (Japanese Omelette)
- **Description**: A fluffy, sweet-and-savory rolled omelette, often served on a stick.
- **Ingredients**: Eggs, sugar, soy sauce, mirin.
- **Where to Find**: Tsukiji Outer Market at stands like Tamagoyaki Shun.
- **Cost**: ¥300-¥500 ($3-$5).
- **Best Times to Visit**: Morning (8 AM - 11 AM).
- **Hygiene/Safety**: Generally safe; opt for vendors with a high turnover.

## Afternoon Snacks

### 3. Yakitori (Grilled Chicken Skewers)
- **Description**: Skewered chicken grilled over an open flame, usually seasoned with salt or tare (a sweet sauce).
- **Ingredients**: Chicken, tare sauce, salt, green onions.
- **Where to Find**: Omoide Yokocho in Shinjuku is famous for its yakitori stalls.
- **Cost**: ¥100-¥300 ($1-$3) per skewer.
- **Best Times to Visit**: Lunch hours (11 AM - 2 PM).
- **Hygiene/Safety**: Look for stalls with fresh ingredients and a busy atmosphere.

### 4. Korokke (Croquettes)
- **Description**: Deep-fried breaded patties filled with mashed potatoes or ground meat.
- **Ingredients**: Potatoes, meat, breadcrumbs.
- **Where to Find**: Popular at street vendors in Harajuku or at the Korokke stand in Ameyoko Market.
- **Cost**: ¥150-¥250 ($1.50-$2.50).
- **Best Times to Visit**: Early afternoon (12 PM - 3 PM).
- **Hygiene/Safety**: Choose vendors that are busy to ensure freshness.

### 5. Kakigori (Shaved Ice)
- **Description**: A refreshing dessert made of finely shaved ice, topped with flavored syrups and condensed milk.
- **Ingredients**: Ice, flavored syrups (like strawberry or matcha), condensed milk.
- **Where to Find**: Shibuya’s Harajuku district, especially during summer at specialty dessert shops.
- **Cost**: ¥500-¥800 ($5-$8).
- **Best Times to Visit**: Hot afternoons (1 PM - 4 PM).
- **Hygiene/Safety**: Look for shops with clear hygiene practices and busy customers.

## Evening Foods

### 6. Takoyaki (Octopus Balls)
- **Description**: Ball-shaped snacks made of batter filled with diced octopus, green onions, and pickled ginger.
- **Ingredients**: Wheat flour, octopus, green onions, bonito flakes.
- **Where to Find**: Dotonbori in Osaka is famous, but you can also find delicious takoyaki in Shinjuku or Asakusa.
- **Cost**: ¥500-¥800 ($5-$8) for a serving.
- **Best Times to Visit**: Evening (5 PM - 10 PM).
- **Hygiene/Safety**: Choose vendors that are busy; they usually have a higher turnover of fresh food.

### 7. Yaki Imo (Roasted Sweet Potato)
- **Description**: Sweet potatoes roasted to perfection, often sold from trucks or stalls.
- **Ingredients**: Sweet potatoes (often the Beni Haruka variety).
- **Where to Find**: Street vendors in Ueno Park and near train stations in autumn.
- **Cost**: ¥200-¥400 ($2-$4).
- **Best Times to Visit**: Fall and winter evenings (5 PM - 8 PM).
- **Hygiene/Safety**: Generally safe due to the high heat used in cooking.

### 8. Ramen
- **Description**: While typically found in restaurants, many vendors offer ramen bowls in street food style.
- **Ingredients**: Noodles, broth (pork, chicken, or vegetarian), toppings (green onions, egg).
- **Where to Find**: Ramen stalls in Akihabara or Shinjuku.
- **Cost**: ¥700-¥1,500 ($7-$15).
- **Best Times to Visit**: Late-night (10 PM - 2 AM).
- **Hygiene/Safety**: Look for busy stalls with good reviews.

## Night Markets & Food Areas

### 9. Tsukiji Outer Market
- **Description**: A vibrant market full of fresh seafood and street food options.
- **Recommended Foods**: Sushi, grilled seafood, and more.
- **Best Times to Visit**: Early morning to late afternoon (5 AM - 3 PM).
- **Hygiene/Safety**: Generally very clean; vendors must adhere to strict hygiene standards.

### 10. Ameyoko Market
- **Description**: A bustling market street known for affordable snacks and street foods.
- **Recommended Foods**: Takoyaki, yakitori, and sweets.
- **Best Times to Visit**: Late afternoon to evening (4 PM - 10 PM).
- **Hygiene/Safety**: Look for busy vendors and those with clear food handling practices.

## Seasonal & Festival Foods

### 11. Hanami Dango (Cherry Blossom Dumplings)
- **Description**: Colorful rice dumplings served on a stick, traditionally enjoyed during cherry blossom viewing (hanami).
- **Ingredients**: Rice flour, sugar, food coloring.
- **When to Find**: March-April during sakura season.
- **Cost**: ¥300-¥500 ($3-$5).
- **Hygiene/Safety**: Generally safe; choose busy vendors.

### 12. Mochi (Rice Cake)
- **Description**: Chewy rice cakes often filled with sweet red bean paste or ice cream.
- **Where to Find**: Street vendors during festivals or at shrines.
