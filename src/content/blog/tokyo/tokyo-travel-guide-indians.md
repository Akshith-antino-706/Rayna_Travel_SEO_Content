# Ultimate Travel Guide for Indian Travelers Visiting Tokyo

Tokyo, Japan's bustling capital, is a blend of ultramodern skyscrapers and traditional temples, making it a captivating destination for travelers from India. Whether you're planning to explore the vibrant neighborhoods, savor the culinary delights, or immerse yourself in the rich culture, this guide will help you navigate your journey with ease.

## Visa Requirements for Indian Travelers

Before embarking on your adventure, ensure that you have the correct visa to enter Japan. Indian travelers need to apply for a short-term tourist visa, which allows for stays of up to 90 days. Here’s how to go about it:

### Steps to Apply for a Tourist Visa:

1. **Documents Required**:
   - Completed visa application form (available at the Japanese embassy website).
   - Recent passport-sized photographs (2).
   - Valid passport (with at least two blank pages).
   - Flight itinerary (round trip reservation).
   - Hotel bookings or a letter of invitation from a host in Japan.
   - Financial proof (bank statements for the last six months).
   - Employment proof or leave letter from your employer.
   - Travel insurance (recommended).

2. **Processing Time**: Typically, it takes about 3-5 working days for the visa to be processed.

3. **Visa Fees**: Approximately ₹6000-₹8000 (subject to change).

### Emergency Embassy Contact Information

In case of emergencies, you can reach out to the Indian Embassy in Tokyo:

- **Address**: 2-2-11, Higashi-Azabu, Minato-ku, Tokyo 106-0044, Japan
- **Phone**: +81-3-5232-3000
- **Email**: cons.tokyo@mea.gov.in

## Indian Food Availability and Dining Preferences

Tokyo boasts a range of Indian restaurants catering to various tastes. Here are some popular options:

- **Garam Masala** (Shinjuku): A well-known spot for authentic North Indian cuisine. Expect to spend around ¥1,500-¥3,000 per meal.
- **Nirvanam** (Shibuya): Famous for its South Indian fare, it offers a cozy ambiance. Prices range from ¥800-¥2,500.
- **Curry House CoCo Ichibanya**: While not Indian per se, this chain offers customizable curry dishes that appeal to Indian palates.

### Dining Tips:
- **Vegetarian/Vegan Options**: Many restaurants in Tokyo offer vegetarian options. Look for "yasai" (vegetable) dishes.
- **Dining Etiquette**: Tipping is not customary in Japan. It may be considered rude. Instead, express gratitude verbally.

## Currency Conversion and Money Management

### Currency Details

The currency used in Japan is the Japanese Yen (JPY). The current exchange rate (as of October 2023) is approximately:

- **1 INR = 1.7 JPY** (subject to fluctuations).

### Money Management Tips

- **ATMs**: Indian travelers can conveniently withdraw cash from ATMs that accept international cards. Look for ATMs at convenience stores like 7-Eleven and FamilyMart, which typically accept Visa, Mastercard, and Cirrus cards.
- **Indian Credit Cards**: Major credit cards such as Visa and Mastercard are widely accepted in Tokyo, especially in restaurants, hotels, and shops. However, it’s advisable to carry cash for smaller establishments.
- **Money Transfer**: Services like Western Union and MoneyGram are available for sending and receiving money. Additionally, consider using international transfer services such as TransferWise (Wise) for better exchange rates and lower fees.

## Cultural Differences to Be Aware Of

### Key Cultural Insights

- **Greetings**: Bowing is a common form of greeting. A polite nod is appreciated, and handshakes are also common.
- **Shoes**: It’s customary to remove shoes when entering homes and some traditional establishments. Look for slippers provided for guests.
- **Public Behavior**: Speaking loudly or making noise in public spaces is frowned upon. Keep conversations at a low volume, especially on trains.
- **Dining**: Slurping noodles is a sign of enjoyment in Japan, so don’t hesitate to join in!

## Travel Documents Needed

Make sure to carry the following documents:

- Passport (valid for at least six months beyond your date of entry).
- Visa (printed copy).
- Travel insurance documentation.
- International driving permit (if you plan to drive).
- Hotel bookings and return flight tickets.

## Tips for Managing Cultural and Dietary Preferences

- **Dietary Management**: If you're vegetarian or have specific dietary requirements, learn some basic Japanese phrases. For example, "Watashi wa bejitarian desu" (I am a vegetarian).
- **Cultural Respect**: Familiarizing yourself with local customs, such as bowing and polite greetings, will enhance your travel experience and foster goodwill.

## Staying Connected to India

### Mobile Connectivity

- **SIM Cards**: Purchase a prepaid SIM card upon arrival. Providers like SoftBank and NTT Docomo offer plans suitable for tourists. Expect to pay around ¥3,000 for a month of data.
- **Wi-Fi**: Many cafes and restaurants offer free Wi-Fi. Look for portable Wi-Fi routers for convenience.

### Indian Communities and Cultural Centers

- **Indian Community**: Tokyo hosts a vibrant Indian community, particularly in neighborhoods like Minato and Shinjuku. You can connect with fellow Indians through social media groups and meetups.
- **Cultural Centers**: The **Indian Cultural Center** in Tokyo often organizes events and activities that celebrate Indian culture. Keep an eye on their schedule for festivals, workshops, and exhibitions.

## Conclusion

Tokyo is a city that welcomes travelers from all over the world, including India. With its rich cultural heritage, delicious food, and friendly locals, your journey is sure to be a memorable one. By preparing ahead and understanding local customs and practices, you can make the most out of your visit. Safe travels!
