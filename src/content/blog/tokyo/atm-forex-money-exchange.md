# Your Ultimate Guide to Accessing Money and Exchanging Currency in Tokyo

Traveling to Tokyo, one of the world's most vibrant cities, can be an exhilarating experience. However, navigating the financial landscape can sometimes prove challenging. This guide will walk you through accessing money and exchanging currency in Tokyo, covering everything from ATM locations and fees to forex exchange options.

## Accessing Money: ATMs in Tokyo

### ATM Locations

Tokyo is equipped with a network of ATMs, making it easy to withdraw cash. Here are some reliable options:

- **7-Eleven Convenience Stores**: These stores are ubiquitous in Tokyo and have ATMs that accept international cards. They are typically open 24/7.
- **FamilyMart**: Another popular convenience store chain with ATMs that accept foreign cards.
- **Post Offices**: Japan Post ATMs also accept foreign cards. They are usually open during business hours, but many are available on weekends.
- **Major Banks**: ATMs in banks like **Mitsubishi UFJ**, **Sumitomo Mitsui**, and **Mizuho** also accept international cards. 

### Fees and Daily Withdrawal Limits

- **Withdrawal Fees**: Expect to pay around ¥200 to ¥300 (approximately $1.50 to $2.25) per transaction when using ATMs to withdraw cash with foreign cards. Your home bank may charge additional fees.
- **Daily Withdrawal Limits**: Most ATMs allow you to withdraw between ¥10,000 to ¥100,000 (about $75 to $750) per transaction. Check with your bank for your specific limits.

### Safety of Using ATMs

Using ATMs in Tokyo is generally safe. However, it's wise to follow these tips:

- Choose ATMs in well-lit, busy areas, like convenience stores or banks.
- Always cover your PIN while entering it.
- Monitor your surroundings while withdrawing cash.
- Be aware of any suspicious behavior around you.

## Forex Exchange Options in Tokyo

### Airport Exchanges

Currency exchange at Tokyo Narita and Haneda airports is convenient but often comes with higher fees. 

- **Narita Airport**: The Travelex counters are located in both Terminals 1 and 2. Expect a lower exchange rate and a commission of around 5% on exchanges.
- **Haneda Airport**: Similar to Narita, Travelex also offers services here, with comparable fees.

### Bank Exchanges

Major banks in Tokyo offer currency exchange services but are typically open during business hours (weekdays from 9 AM to 3 PM). 

- **Mitsubishi UFJ Bank**: Located throughout the city, they offer competitive rates and minimal commission fees.
- **Sumitomo Mitsui Banking Corporation**: Another major bank with good exchange services available at various locations.

### Street Exchangers

Street currency exchange shops can be found in areas like Shinjuku and Shibuya. While they may offer better rates, be cautious:

- **Exchange Rates**: These can fluctuate, and rates might not be as favorable as banks.
- **Commission Transparency**: Ensure you understand any commissions before proceeding with the exchange.

### Comparison of Costs

| Method           | Exchange Rate | Commission                      | Pros                      | Cons                        |
|------------------|---------------|---------------------------------|---------------------------|-----------------------------|
| Airport Exchange  | Low           | ~5%                             | Convenient                | Higher fees                 |
| Bank Exchange     | Moderate      | Low (around 1-2%)               | Reliable, trustworthy     | Limited hours               |
| Street Exchanger  | Variable      | Varies, often unclear           | Potentially better rates  | Risk of scams               |

## Tips for Getting the Best Rates

1. **Avoid Airport Exchanges**: Unless absolutely necessary, avoid exchanging currency at airports.
2. **Use ATMs**: Withdrawing cash directly from ATMs generally offers better rates than exchanges.
3. **Check Online Rates**: Websites like XE.com provide real-time exchange rates. Use this as a benchmark when exchanging currency.
4. **Negotiate**: If using street exchangers, don’t hesitate to negotiate the rate.

## Keeping Money Safe

- **Divvy Up Cash**: Carry a small amount of cash and keep larger sums secured in a hotel safe.
- **Use RFID-Blocking Wallets**: These can protect your cards from electronic theft.
- **Regularly Monitor Bank Statements**: Keep an eye on your accounts for any unauthorized transactions.

## Carrying Cash vs. Cards

### Cash

- **Cash is King**: While Tokyo is increasingly accepting credit cards, many smaller establishments, local restaurants, and markets still only accept cash.
- **Recommended Amount**: Carry around ¥5,000 to ¥10,000 (about $40 to $75) for daily expenses.

### Cards

- **Credit/Debit Cards**: Visa and Mastercard are widely accepted. American Express is less commonly used.
- **Notify Your Bank**: Before traveling, inform your bank of your travel plans to avoid any blocks on your card.

## International Transfer Options

If you need to transfer money while in Tokyo, consider these options:

- **TransferWise (now Wise)**: Offers competitive exchange rates and low fees for international transfers.
- **PayPal**: Useful for transferring money, but be aware of higher fees and less favorable exchange rates.
- **Western Union**: Available for cash transfers, but check for fees and exchange rates before proceeding.

## Final Thoughts

Accessing money and exchanging currency in Tokyo can be straightforward if you know where to go and what to expect. Keep the tips in this guide in mind to ensure that your financial transactions are smooth and secure during your stay in this captivating city. Whether you're dining in a local izakaya or shopping in Ginza, being financially savvy will enhance your travel experience in Tokyo. Happy travels!
