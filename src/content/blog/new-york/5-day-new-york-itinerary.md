# 5-Day New York City Itinerary

New York City, the Big Apple, is a vibrant metropolis filled with iconic landmarks, diverse neighborhoods, and abundant cultural experiences. This comprehensive 5-day itinerary will guide you through the best of NYC, balancing main attractions with local experiences.

---

## Day 1: Iconic Landmarks & Times Square

### Morning
- **8:00 AM - Breakfast at Ess-a-Bagel**  
  Enjoy a classic New York breakfast with a bagel and cream cheese. Prices range from $3 to $10.  
  Location: 831 3rd Ave.

- **9:00 AM - Visit the Empire State Building**  
  Duration: 1.5 hours  
  **Tip**: Buy tickets online ($44) to skip the lines. Visit the 86th-floor observation deck for stunning views.

### Afternoon
- **11:00 AM - Walk to Bryant Park**  
  Duration: 30 minutes  
  Take a leisurely stroll through Midtown and enjoy the park's beauty.

- **11:30 AM - Lunch at The Modern**  
  Enjoy a contemporary meal at this Michelin-starred restaurant. Average cost: $40 per person.  
  Location: 9 W 53rd St.

- **1:00 PM - Explore the Museum of Modern Art (MoMA)**  
  Duration: 2 hours  
  Admission: $25. Discover world-renowned artworks.

### Evening
- **3:30 PM - Stroll through Times Square**  
  Duration: 1 hour  
  Immerse yourself in the bright lights and bustling atmosphere.

- **5:00 PM - Dinner at Carmine's**  
  Indulge in family-style Italian dining. Average cost: $30 per person.  
  Location: 200 W 44th St.

- **7:00 PM - Broadway Show**  
  Duration: 2.5 hours  
  Choose from popular shows like "The Lion King" or "Hamilton." Tickets vary significantly; average $100-200.

### Transportation
- Use the subway (MTA) for efficient travel. A MetroCard costs $1, with rides at $2.75.

---

## Day 2: Central Park & Cultural Institutions

### Morning
- **8:00 AM - Breakfast at Sarabeth's**  
  Enjoy a hearty breakfast. Average cost: $20 per person.  
  Location: Central Park South.

- **9:00 AM - Central Park Exploration**  
  Duration: 2 hours  
  Visit iconic spots like Bethesda Terrace and Bow Bridge. Rent a bike for $15/hour to cover more ground.

### Afternoon
- **11:00 AM - Visit the American Museum of Natural History**  
  Duration: 2.5 hours  
  Admission: Suggested donation of $23. Explore fascinating exhibits, including the dinosaur gallery.

- **1:30 PM - Lunch at Shake Shack**  
  Enjoy a delicious burger and fries. Average cost: $15.  
  Location: Columbus Circle.

### Evening
- **3:00 PM - The Metropolitan Museum of Art**  
  Duration: 2.5 hours  
  Admission: Suggested donation of $25. Explore one of the world's largest art collections. 

- **5:30 PM - Dinner at The Smith**  
  Average cost: $30 per person.  
  Location: 1900 Broadway.

- **7:30 PM - Evening stroll along the High Line**  
  Duration: 1 hour  
  Enjoy the sunset and unique views of the city.

### Transportation
- The subway is the best option. Consider walking for short distances to enjoy the city views.

---

## Day 3: Neighborhood Exploration

### Morning
- **8:00 AM - Breakfast at Jack's Wife Freda**  
  A cozy spot offering Mediterranean-American dishes. Average cost: $20.  
  Location: 224 Lafayette St.

- **9:30 AM - Explore SoHo**  
  Duration: 2 hours  
  Shop in trendy boutiques and enjoy street art.

### Afternoon
- **12:00 PM - Lunch at Prince Street Pizza**  
  Famous for its pepperoni slices. Average cost: $5 per slice.  
  Location: 27 Prince St.

- **1:00 PM - Walk to Greenwich Village**  
  Duration: 1 hour  
  Explore Washington Square Park and the charming streets.

- **2:00 PM - Visit The Stonewall Inn**  
  Duration: 1 hour  
  Learn about the LGBTQ+ history of this iconic site.

### Evening
- **3:00 PM - Explore the West Village**  
  Duration: 2 hours  
  Discover unique shops and cafés. 

- **5:30 PM - Dinner at The Spotted Pig**  
  Enjoy gastropub fare. Average cost: $30.  
  Location: 314 W 11th St.

- **7:00 PM - Jazz at The Blue Note**  
  Duration: 2 hours  
  Admission varies by performance; average $30. Experience live jazz in an intimate setting.

### Transportation
- Walking is ideal in these neighborhoods. The subway can be used for longer distances.

---

## Day 4: Day Trip to Brooklyn

### Morning
- **8:00 AM - Breakfast at Butler**  
  A local favorite known for its pastries. Average cost: $15.  
  Location: Williamsburg, Brooklyn.

- **9:30 AM - Explore Brooklyn Bridge**  
  Duration: 1.5 hours  
  Walk the iconic bridge for stunning views of Manhattan.

### Afternoon
- **11:00 AM - Visit DUMBO**  
  Duration: 1 hour  
  Explore this trendy area and take photos at the Manhattan Bridge view.

- **12:00 PM - Lunch at Time Out Market**  
  Enjoy various food offerings from local vendors. Average cost: $20.  
  Location: DUMBO.

- **1:30 PM - Brooklyn Heights Promenade**  
  Duration: 1 hour  
  Stroll along the waterfront with spectacular skyline views.

### Evening
- **3:00 PM - Brooklyn Botanic Garden**  
  Duration: 1.5 hours  
  Admission: $18. Experience beautiful gardens and seasonal blooms.

- **5:00 PM - Dinner at Juliana's Pizza**  
  Known for its coal-fired pizza. Average cost: $25.  
  Location: 19 Old Fulton St.

- **7:00 PM - Return to Manhattan**  
  Take the subway back for a relaxed evening.

### Transportation
- The subway is the best option for crossing to Brooklyn. Walking is ideal for local exploration.
