# 3-Day Paris Itinerary: Explore the City of Light Like a Local

Paris, the City of Light, is a treasure trove of culture, history, and culinary delights. This 3-day itinerary will help you discover the most iconic attractions, dive into local neighborhoods, and experience unique activities that cater to various interests and budgets.

---

## Day 1: Main Tourist Attractions and Central Areas

### Morning: Iconic Landmarks

**8:00 AM - Breakfast at Café de Flore**  
Start your day with a classic Parisian breakfast at Café de Flore. Enjoy a croissant and café crème for around €10. This historic café has been frequented by intellectuals and artists, adding to its charm.

**9:00 AM - Visit the Eiffel Tower**  
Head to the Eiffel Tower (Champ de Mars, 5 Avenue Anatole France) by 9 AM to beat the crowds. Book your tickets online in advance to skip the line. Prices start at €26 for adults. Take the elevator to the summit for stunning views of the city.

### Midday: Explore the Seine and Notre-Dame

**11:00 AM - Stroll Along the Seine**  
Walk along the Seine River and soak in the views. Don’t forget to stop at the iconic Pont Alexandre III for photos.

**12:00 PM - Lunch at Le Relais de l’Entrecôte**  
Enjoy a classic French steak-frites lunch (around €23) at Le Relais de l’Entrecôte, famous for its secret sauce.

**1:30 PM - Notre-Dame Cathedral**  
Visit Notre-Dame Cathedral (6 Parvis Notre-Dame - Pl. Jean-Paul II) and admire its Gothic architecture. Although the interior is still under restoration, the exterior is breathtaking. Entry is free.

### Afternoon: The Heart of Paris

**2:30 PM - Sainte-Chapelle**  
Just a short walk from Notre-Dame is Sainte-Chapelle (8 Boulevard du Palais). Don’t miss the stunning stained-glass windows. Entry is €11.50.

**4:00 PM - Explore the Latin Quarter**  
Wander through the narrow streets of the Latin Quarter. Stop by Shakespeare and Company, a historic bookstore with a cozy café.

### Evening: Dinner and Entertainment

**6:00 PM - Dinner at L’As du Fallafel**  
Enjoy a delicious falafel wrap (around €7) at L’As du Fallafel in the Marais, a favorite among locals.

**8:00 PM - Seine River Cruise**  
End your day with a scenic Seine River cruise. Bateaux Parisiens offers evening cruises starting at €15, providing a unique perspective of illuminated landmarks.

### Insider Tip:
Use the Paris Metro to save time. A carnet (book of 10 tickets) costs around €16.90, making it more economical for multiple trips.

---

## Day 2: Neighborhood Exploration and Authentic Dining

### Morning: Montmartre Magic

**8:30 AM - Breakfast at Le Consulat**  
Start your day in Montmartre with breakfast at Le Consulat. A typical breakfast will cost around €8.

**9:30 AM - Visit Sacré-Cœur Basilica**  
Climb up to Sacré-Cœur Basilica (35 Rue du Chevalier de la Barre) for panoramic views of Paris. Entry is free, but the dome access costs €6.

### Midday: Artistic Exploration

**11:00 AM - Explore Montmartre’s Art Scene**  
Wander the charming streets of Montmartre. Visit Place du Tertre, where artists showcase their work. For a unique experience, stop by the Musée de l'Orangerie (Jardin des Tuileries, €9) to see Monet’s Water Lilies.

**12:30 PM - Lunch at Le Refuge des Fondus**  
For an authentic experience, enjoy fondue (around €25) at Le Refuge des Fondus. Don’t forget to try their unique wine served in baby bottles!

### Afternoon: Local Markets and Shopping

**2:30 PM - Visit Rue Cler Market**  
Head to Rue Cler, a famous market street. Sample artisanal cheese, fresh bread, and pastries. Be sure to grab a baguette from Maison Landemaine.

**4:00 PM - Shopping in Le Marais**  
Explore the boutiques and vintage shops in Le Marais. Check out Merci, a concept store with a unique selection of clothing, home goods, and a lovely café.

### Evening: Dinner and Nightlife

**6:30 PM - Dinner at Chez Janou**  
Indulge in Provençal cuisine at Chez Janou (2 Rue Roger Verlomme), where meals start at €30. Save room for their famous chocolate mousse!

**9:00 PM - Cabaret Show at Le Moulin Rouge**  
Experience a classic Parisian cabaret show at Le Moulin Rouge. Tickets start from €87, including dinner options, so book in advance.

### Insider Tip:
Keep an eye out for local art exhibits or pop-up markets in Montmartre and Le Marais, which often feature local artists and artisans.

---

## Day 3: Specialized Experiences

### Morning: Day Trip to Versailles

**8:00 AM - Breakfast at Angelina**  
Start your day with a hot chocolate and pastry at Angelina (226 Rue de Rivoli). Expect to pay around €15.

**9:00 AM - Day Trip to Versailles**  
Head to the RER C train at Gare Saint-Lazare, which takes about 40 minutes to reach the Palace of Versailles. Entry (including gardens) is €20. Make sure to book your tickets in advance.

### Midday: Explore the Palace and Gardens

**11:00 AM - Tour the Palace**  
Explore the Hall of Mirrors and the royal apartments. Don’t miss the stunning gardens, where you can rent a bike for €5 to explore further.

**1:00 PM - Lunch at La Petite Venise**  
Enjoy a leisurely lunch in the gardens at La Petite Venise, where meals range from €15-€25.

### Afternoon: Cultural Immersion

**3:00 PM - Visit the Trianons**  
After lunch, visit the Grand Trianon and the Petit Trianon, offering a glimpse into the lifestyle of French royalty.

**5:00 PM - Return to Paris**  
Head back to Paris and relax at your hotel or explore a nearby café.

### Evening: Luxury Dining and Nightlife

**7:30 PM - Dinner at Le Meurice**  
Experience fine dining at Le Meurice, where a meal can start at €90. The exquisite atmosphere and Michelin-starred cuisine make it a memorable experience.

**9:30 PM - Le Bar at Le Meurice**  
Enjoy a nightcap at Le Bar, known for its creative cocktails and elegant ambiance. A cocktail will set you back about €20.

### Insider Tip:
If you’re visiting during the summer, check for night fountain shows at Versailles, which can be an enchanting way to see the gardens illuminated at night.
