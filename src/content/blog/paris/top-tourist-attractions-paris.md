# The Ultimate Guide to Paris’s Top Tourist Attractions

Paris, the City of Light, is a treasure trove of history, art, and culture. With its iconic landmarks and vibrant neighborhoods, it’s no wonder that millions of visitors flock to the French capital each year. This guide will take you through the top tourist attractions, organized by area, complete with essential information to enhance your experience.

## Central Paris Attractions

### 1. Eiffel Tower
- **History**: Built for the 1889 Exposition Universelle, the Eiffel Tower is a symbol of French innovation and artistry.
- **Significance**: An architectural marvel, it stands as the most recognizable landmark in the world.
- **Opening Hours**: Daily from 9:30 AM to 11:45 PM (last elevator ascent).
- **Admission Fees**: €16.60 (adult) for the summit, €10.40 for the second floor.
- **Best Time to Visit**: Early morning or late evening to avoid crowds.
- **Time Required**: 1.5 to 2 hours.
- **Facilities**: Restaurants, gift shops, and restrooms.
- **Photography Guidelines**: Great views from the Trocadéro Gardens and on the tower itself.
- **Practical Tips**: Consider booking a **skip-the-line ticket** online to avoid long queues. Guided tours often include access to the summit.

### 2. Louvre Museum
- **History**: Originally a fortress, the Louvre became a royal palace and finally a public museum in 1793.
- **Significance**: Home to over 35,000 works, including the Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo.
- **Opening Hours**: Wednesday to Monday, 9 AM to 6 PM; closed Tuesdays.
- **Admission Fees**: €17 online; €15 at the door.
- **Best Time to Visit**: Late afternoon on weekdays.
- **Time Required**: 3 to 4 hours.
- **Facilities**: Cafés, gift shops, and restrooms.
- **Photography Guidelines**: No flash photography allowed.
- **Practical Tips**: Pre-book tickets to skip the long lines. Consider a guided tour to learn about the key artworks.

### 3. Notre-Dame Cathedral
- **History**: Construction began in 1163, making it a prime example of French Gothic architecture.
- **Significance**: A UNESCO World Heritage site, it plays a pivotal role in French culture and history.
- **Opening Hours**: Currently closed for restoration until further notice.
- **Admission Fees**: Free (once reopened).
- **Best Time to Visit**: Early morning or late afternoon.
- **Time Required**: 1 hour.
- **Facilities**: Gift shop, restrooms.
- **Photography Guidelines**: Best taken from the adjacent Île de la Cité.
- **Practical Tips**: Check for updates on reopening and consider exploring the nearby Sainte-Chapelle for its stunning stained glass.

## Montmartre and Surroundings

### 4. Sacré-Cœur Basilica
- **History**: Completed in 1914, this basilica was built as a symbol of national penance.
- **Significance**: Its dome offers one of the best panoramic views of Paris.
- **Opening Hours**: Daily from 6 AM to 10:30 PM.
- **Admission Fees**: Free (dome access costs €6).
- **Best Time to Visit**: Early morning for fewer crowds.
- **Time Required**: 1 to 1.5 hours.
- **Facilities**: Restrooms, small shops.
- **Photography Guidelines**: Capture the basilica at sunrise for magical lighting.
- **Practical Tips**: Wear comfortable shoes as the climb up the steps can be steep. 

### 5. Moulin Rouge
- **History**: Established in 1889, it’s famous for its can-can dancing and vibrant performances.
- **Significance**: A symbol of the bohemian lifestyle of the late 19th century.
- **Opening Hours**: Shows at 9 PM and 11 PM, restaurant service from 7 PM.
- **Admission Fees**: From €87 (show only) to €270 (dinner and show).
- **Best Time to Visit**: Evening for the full experience.
- **Time Required**: 2 to 3 hours.
- **Facilities**: Restaurant, bar.
- **Photography Guidelines**: Photos are prohibited during the show.
- **Practical Tips**: Book tickets in advance for the best seats. Dress code is smart casual.

## The Latin Quarter and Surroundings

### 6. Panthéon
- **History**: Originally a church, it became a mausoleum for French heroes like Voltaire and Rousseau.
- **Significance**: An architectural masterpiece symbolizing the French Republic.
- **Opening Hours**: Tuesday to Sunday, 10 AM to 6 PM; closed Mondays.
- **Admission Fees**: €11.50.
- **Best Time to Visit**: Mid-morning.
- **Time Required**: 1 hour.
- **Facilities**: Restrooms, gift shop.
- **Photography Guidelines**: Photography is allowed inside.
- **Practical Tips**: Take the stairs to the dome for exceptional views of Paris.

### 7. Luxembourg Gardens
- **History**: Created in 1612 by Marie de’ Medici, the gardens reflect Italian Renaissance-style.
- **Significance**: A serene space in the heart of Paris, perfect for relaxation.
- **Opening Hours**: Daily from 7:30 AM to dusk.
- **Admission Fees**: Free.
- **Best Time to Visit**: Spring for blooming flowers.
- **Time Required**: 1 to 2 hours.
- **Facilities**: Restrooms, playgrounds, café.
- **Photography Guidelines**: Capture the Medici Fountain and the flowerbeds.
- **Practical Tips**: Bring a picnic to enjoy on the lawns.

## La Défense and Modern Paris

### 8. La Défense
- **History**: Established in the 1960s, this district represents modern architecture and business.
- **Significance**: It’s home to numerous skyscrapers and the Grande Arche, a modern version of the Arc de Triomphe.
- **Opening Hours**: Open 24/7.
- **Admission Fees**: Free to explore; some attractions may charge.
- **Best Time to Visit**: Late afternoon for sunset views.
- **Time Required**: 1 to 2 hours.
- **Facilities**: Shops, restaurants.
- **Photography Guidelines**: Capture the Grande Arche from different angles.
- **Practical Tips**: Combine with a visit to the nearby Parc André Citroën for a unique contrast.
