# The Ultimate Guide to the Top Things to Do in Paris

Paris, the City of Light, offers an incredible array of experiences for tourists. From iconic landmarks and rich cultural sites to stunning parks and unique modern attractions, there's something for everyone. Here’s a comprehensive list of the top 20 things to do in Paris, organized by category, including must-dos, practical tips, and insider advice.

## Iconic Landmarks

### 1. **Eiffel Tower**
- **Description**: The quintessential symbol of Paris, the Eiffel Tower offers breathtaking views of the city.
- **Estimated Duration**: 2-3 hours.
- **Best Time to Visit**: Early morning or late evening to avoid crowds.
- **Approximate Cost**: €16.60 for the summit ticket.
- **Practical Tip**: Book tickets online in advance to skip the long lines. Visiting during sunset provides stunning views.

### 2. **Louvre Museum**
- **Description**: Home to thousands of works of art, including the Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo.
- **Estimated Duration**: 3-5 hours.
- **Best Time to Visit**: Wednesday and Friday evenings (open until 9:45 PM).
- **Approximate Cost**: €17 for a timed entry ticket.
- **Practical Tip**: Download the Louvre app for a self-guided tour. Prioritize the Denon Wing for the highlights.

### 3. **Notre-Dame Cathedral**
- **Description**: This Gothic masterpiece is known for its stunning architecture and history.
- **Estimated Duration**: 1-2 hours.
- **Best Time to Visit**: Early morning or late afternoon.
- **Approximate Cost**: Free (currently closed for restoration; check updates).
- **Practical Tip**: Explore the Île de la Cité for a complete experience.

### 4. **Sacré-Cœur Basilica**
- **Description**: Located on the highest point in the city, this basilica offers panoramic views of Paris.
- **Estimated Duration**: 1-2 hours.
- **Best Time to Visit**: Early morning or sunset.
- **Approximate Cost**: Free to enter (climbing the dome is €6).
- **Practical Tip**: Avoid the midday crowds by visiting during off-peak hours.

## Cultural Experiences

### 5. **Montmartre and the Artists’ Square**
- **Description**: A historic district known for its bohemian vibe and artists.
- **Estimated Duration**: 2-3 hours.
- **Best Time to Visit**: Weekdays for fewer tourists.
- **Approximate Cost**: Free to explore.
- **Practical Tip**: Visit the Place du Tertre to see artists at work and consider purchasing a small piece as a souvenir.

### 6. **Musée d'Orsay**
- **Description**: An art museum housed in a former railway station, focusing on Impressionist and Post-Impressionist masterpieces.
- **Estimated Duration**: 2-3 hours.
- **Best Time to Visit**: Thursday and Friday evenings.
- **Approximate Cost**: €16 for a ticket.
- **Practical Tip**: Opt for a guided tour to gain deeper insights into the artworks.

### 7. **Palace of Versailles**
- **Description**: A short train ride from Paris, the opulent palace and gardens are a UNESCO World Heritage site.
- **Estimated Duration**: Full day (8 hours).
- **Best Time to Visit**: Weekdays, especially in spring and fall.
- **Approximate Cost**: €20 for the palace and gardens.
- **Practical Tip**: Rent a bike to explore the expansive gardens.

## Natural Attractions

### 8. **Luxembourg Gardens**
- **Description**: A beautiful park perfect for a leisurely stroll, picnics, or enjoying the scenery.
- **Estimated Duration**: 1-2 hours.
- **Best Time to Visit**: Spring and summer for blooming flowers.
- **Approximate Cost**: Free.
- **Practical Tip**: Check for outdoor concerts or events during your visit.

### 9. **Seine River Cruise**
- **Description**: A scenic boat ride along the Seine, offering views of Parisian landmarks.
- **Estimated Duration**: 1 hour.
- **Best Time to Visit**: Evening for a romantic experience.
- **Approximate Cost**: €15-€20 for a basic cruise.
- **Practical Tip**: Consider a dinner cruise for a memorable dining experience.

## Modern Attractions

### 10. **Centre Pompidou**
- **Description**: A modern art museum known for its radical architecture and vast collection.
- **Estimated Duration**: 2-3 hours.
- **Best Time to Visit**: Weekdays.
- **Approximate Cost**: €15.
- **Practical Tip**: Don’t miss the rooftop terrace for stunning views of the city.

### 11. **La Défense**
- **Description**: A district known for its modern skyscrapers and shopping centers.
- **Estimated Duration**: 1-2 hours.
- **Best Time to Visit**: During the day for full effect.
- **Approximate Cost**: Free to explore.
- **Practical Tip**: Visit the Grande Arche for a unique perspective of Paris.

## Unique Experiences

### 12. **Cooking Class**
- **Description**: Learn to cook classic French dishes with a local chef.
- **Estimated Duration**: 3-4 hours.
- **Best Time to Visit**: Classes available year-round.
- **Approximate Cost**: €70-€150 depending on the class.
- **Practical Tip**: Book a class that includes a market visit for sourcing fresh ingredients.

### 13. **Catacombs of Paris**
- **Description**: A haunting underground ossuary that holds the remains of over six million Parisians.
- **Estimated Duration**: 1-2 hours.
- **Best Time to Visit**: Weekdays.
- **Approximate Cost**: €14.
- **Practical Tip**: Buy tickets online to avoid long waits.

## Seasonal Attractions

### 14. **Christmas Markets (Winter)**
- **Description**: Festive markets with crafts, food, and mulled wine.
- **Estimated Duration**: 1-2 hours per market.
- **Best Time to Visit**: Late November to early January.
- **Approximate Cost**: Free entry; pay for food and crafts.
- **Practical Tip**: Visit the market at Les Halles for a large selection.

### 15. **Paris Plages (Summer)**
- **Description**: Temporary beaches along the Seine, complete with sand and lounge chairs.
- **Estimated Duration**: 1-3 hours.
- **Best Time to Visit**: July to August.
- **Approximate Cost**: Free.
- **Practical Tip**: Bring a book and enjoy a relaxing day by the river.
