# Visitor's Guide to the Eiffel Tower in Paris

The Eiffel Tower is not only a symbol of Paris but also an iconic representation of France. This majestic structure offers breathtaking views of the city and an unforgettable experience. In this guide, we’ll delve into the history, what to expect, and how to make the most of your visit to this monumental attraction.

## History of the Eiffel Tower

Constructed between 1887 and 1889, the Eiffel Tower was designed by engineer Gustave Eiffel as the centerpiece for the 1889 Exposition Universelle (World's Fair) held to celebrate the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution. Standing at 1,083 feet tall, it was the tallest man-made structure in the world until the completion of the Chrysler Building in New York City in 1930. Originally met with criticism, the Eiffel Tower has become one of the most recognizable structures globally, attracting millions of visitors each year.

## What You'll See and Experience

When you visit the Eiffel Tower, you can explore three different levels:

### Level 1
- **Glass Floor**: Experience a thrilling view of the ground below.
- **Exhibits**: Learn about the tower's history and construction through interactive displays.
- **Shops and Restaurants**: Enjoy a quick bite or purchase souvenirs.

### Level 2
- **Panoramic Views**: A fantastic viewpoint where you can see landmarks like the Seine River, the Louvre, and Montmartre.
- **Dining**: Dine at the 58 Tour Eiffel restaurant for a unique meal.

### Level 3
- **Summit Observation Deck**: The highest point accessible to the public, offering stunning 360-degree views of Paris.
- **Champagne Bar**: Sip champagne while enjoying unparalleled views.

## Recommended Duration

Allocate **2 to 3 hours** for your visit. This allows time for exploration, enjoying views, and perhaps a meal.

## Best Times to Visit

- **Early Morning**: Arrive just after opening (9:30 AM) to avoid crowds.
- **Late Afternoon**: Visit in the late afternoon for beautiful sunset views and to see the tower illuminated at night.

## Ticket Options and Prices

- **Standard Tickets**: 
  - Adults: €26.10 for access to the summit.
  - Youth (12-24 years): €13.00.
  - Children (4-11 years): €6.50.
- **Elevator Access**: Pre-book tickets online to skip the long queues.
- **Guided Tours**: Tickets start at around €45, including a guided experience.

## How to Get There

### Public Transportation
- **Metro**: Take Line 6 to Bir-Hakeim or Line 9 to Trocadéro.
- **RER**: Use Line C to Champ de Mars - Tour Eiffel.

### Parking
- Limited street parking is available. The **Parking du Trocadéro** is a nearby option, costing about €3.50 per hour.

## Accessibility Information

The Eiffel Tower is wheelchair accessible. The elevators are equipped to accommodate wheelchair users, and there are accessible restrooms available.

## Dining and Bathroom Facilities

- **Dining Options**: 
  - **58 Tour Eiffel** (reservations highly recommended).
  - **Buffet and Cafeteria** on Level 1 for quick snacks.
- **Restrooms**: Available on all levels, with accessible facilities.

## Photography Rules

- Photography is allowed throughout the tower. However, be considerate of others and avoid using tripods unless permitted.
- The best photo spots include:
  - **Trocadéro Gardens**: Iconic views of the tower.
  - **Champ de Mars**: Great for capturing the tower's full height.
  - The **summit observation deck** offers stunning panoramic shots.

## Guided Tour Options

Consider booking a guided tour for a deeper understanding of the Eiffel Tower's history and architecture. Options include:
- **Skip-the-Line Tours**: Save time and enhance your experience with a knowledgeable guide.
- **Evening Tours**: Enjoy the beauty of the tower lit up at night.

## Insider Tips for Avoiding Crowds

- **Visit During Off-Peak Days**: Weekdays are generally less crowded than weekends.
- **Time Your Visit**: Aim for early morning or late afternoon visits.
- **Pre-book Tickets**: This will save you time and help you avoid long queues.

## Must-See Sections

- **The Glass Floor** on Level 1 for a thrilling view.
- **The Summit Observation Deck** for breathtaking panoramas of Paris.
- **The 58 Tour Eiffel Restaurant** for a special dining experience.

## Nearby Attractions

- **Champ de Mars**: A beautiful park ideal for picnics and leisurely strolls.
- **Trocadéro Gardens**: Excellent for photography and enjoying the fountain displays.
- **Seine River Cruises**: Take a scenic boat ride along the Seine for a different perspective of the Eiffel Tower and other landmarks.

### Combining Visits

Consider visiting the **Musée du Quai Branly** for a unique art experience or the **Palais de Chaillot** for more picturesque views of the Eiffel Tower. Both attractions are within walking distance.

## Practical Details

### Transportation
- **Metro Station**: Bir-Hakeim is the closest station.
- **Bus Routes**: Lines 42 and 82 stop nearby.

### Nearby Facilities
- Several cafes and shops in the vicinity offer refreshments and souvenirs.

## Conclusion

The Eiffel Tower is a must-visit when in Paris, offering history, breathtaking views, and memorable experiences. By planning your visit carefully, utilizing insider tips, and exploring nearby attractions, you can make the most of this iconic landmark. Whether it's your first time or a return visit, the Eiffel Tower never fails to impress. Enjoy your visit!
