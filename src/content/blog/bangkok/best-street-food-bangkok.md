# A Street Food Lover's Guide to Bangkok

Bangkok is a culinary paradise, especially for street food enthusiasts. The city pulsates with life and flavor, offering a diverse array of dishes that reflect its rich cultural tapestry. This guide will help you navigate the bustling streets and discover 10-15 iconic street foods, along with practical tips to enhance your experience.

## Morning Delights: Breakfast Specialties

### 1. **Jok (Rice Porridge)**
- **Description**: A warm, comforting rice porridge often served with minced pork, ginger, and fried garlic.
- **Main Ingredients**: Rice, pork, ginger, scallions, and soy sauce.
- **Where to Find**: Look for stalls in the Chinatown area (Yaowarat) or at local morning markets like Or Tor Kor Market.
- **Estimated Cost**: 30-50 THB ($1-$1.50).
- **Best Time to Visit**: 6 AM - 10 AM.
- **Hygiene Tip**: Choose busy stalls with a high turnover of food.

### 2. **Khao Tom (Rice Soup)**
- **Description**: A savory soup made with rice and various toppings, often served with fresh herbs and lime.
- **Main Ingredients**: Rice, pork or shrimp, garlic, and fresh herbs.
- **Where to Find**: Street vendors around Silom and Sukhumvit.
- **Estimated Cost**: 40-60 THB ($1.20-$1.80).
- **Best Time to Visit**: 6 AM - 11 AM.
- **Hygiene Tip**: Opt for vendors with clean cooking areas and a queue of locals.

## Midday Munchies: Afternoon Snacks

### 3. **Som Tum (Papaya Salad)**
- **Description**: A spicy salad made from shredded green papaya, tomatoes, peanuts, and chili.
- **Main Ingredients**: Green papaya, tomatoes, peanuts, lime, and fish sauce.
- **Where to Find**: Street stalls in the Chatuchak area or the Old City.
- **Estimated Cost**: 50-80 THB ($1.50-$2.40).
- **Best Time to Visit**: 11 AM - 3 PM.
- **Hygiene Tip**: Look for stalls with fresh ingredients on display.

### 4. **Moo Pad Krapow (Stir-Fried Basil Pork)**
- **Description**: Spicy stir-fried pork with holy basil served over rice and topped with a fried egg.
- **Main Ingredients**: Ground pork, holy basil, chili, and rice.
- **Where to Find**: Vendors near university areas like Thammasat or the streets around Khao San Road.
- **Estimated Cost**: 60-80 THB ($1.80-$2.40).
- **Best Time to Visit**: 11 AM - 4 PM.
- **Hygiene Tip**: Choose vendors with a busy lunch crowd.

### 5. **Satay (Grilled Skewers)**
- **Description**: Grilled meat skewers, often served with peanut sauce and cucumber salad.
- **Main Ingredients**: Chicken or pork, marinated with spices, and served with peanut sauce.
- **Where to Find**: Street vendors in Sukhumvit and along the riverside.
- **Estimated Cost**: 10-20 THB per skewer ($0.30-$0.60).
- **Best Time to Visit**: 2 PM - 5 PM.
- **Hygiene Tip**: Look for vendors with fresh-looking meat and a cooking area kept clean.

## Evening Eats: Night Markets and Food Stalls

### 6. **Pad Thai**
- **Description**: Thailand's famous stir-fried noodle dish, usually made with shrimp or chicken, bean sprouts, and peanuts.
- **Main Ingredients**: Rice noodles, shrimp or chicken, eggs, bean sprouts, and peanuts.
- **Where to Find**: Thip Samai Pad Thai near the Old City or at night markets like Rot Fai.
- **Estimated Cost**: 60-100 THB ($1.80-$3).
- **Best Time to Visit**: 6 PM - 10 PM.
- **Hygiene Tip**: Seek out popular stalls with a line of customers.

### 7. **Khao Niew Mamuang (Mango Sticky Rice)**
- **Description**: A sweet dessert made from glutinous rice, fresh mango slices, and coconut milk.
- **Main Ingredients**: Glutinous rice, mango, and coconut milk.
- **Where to Find**: Street vendors in the Sukhumvit area or at night markets.
- **Estimated Cost**: 50-80 THB ($1.50-$2.40).
- **Best Time to Visit**: After dinner, around 8 PM.
- **Hygiene Tip**: Choose stalls that prepare food in front of you.

### 8. **Nam Tok Moo (Waterfall Pork Salad)**
- **Description**: Grilled pork salad with fresh herbs and lime dressing, known for its zesty flavor.
- **Main Ingredients**: Grilled pork, mint, shallots, and lime.
- **Where to Find**: Street stalls in the Silom area or at various night markets.
- **Estimated Cost**: 60-100 THB ($1.80-$3).
- **Best Time to Visit**: 6 PM - 11 PM.
- **Hygiene Tip**: Look for busy stalls and check for fresh ingredients.

## Iconic Snacks and Seasonal Foods

### 9. **Roti (Thai Pancake)**
- **Description**: A thin pancake filled with banana and condensed milk, then fried until crispy.
- **Main Ingredients**: Flour, banana, and condensed milk.
- **Where to Find**: Street vendors in the Khao San Road area.
- **Estimated Cost**: 30-50 THB ($1-$1.50).
- **Best Time to Visit**: Late evening, around 9 PM.
- **Hygiene Tip**: Choose vendors who cook in front of you.

### 10. **Khanom Krok (Coconut Pudding)**
- **Description**: Mini coconut puddings made in a cast-iron pan, crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.
- **Main Ingredients**: Rice flour, coconut milk, and sugar.
- **Where to Find**: Chatuchak Market or street vendors in the Old City.
- **Estimated Cost**: 20-30 THB for a serving ($0.60-$1).
- **Best Time to Visit**: Morning or late afternoon.
- **Hygiene Tip**: Look for vendors with clean cooking equipment and fresh ingredients.

## Tips for Enjoying Street Food in Bangkok

### Hygiene and Safety
- **Choose Busy Stalls**: High turnover indicates fresh food. 
- **Look for Cleanliness**: Check the cleanliness of cooking areas.
- **Street Food Safety**: Stick to freshly cooked items and avoid raw foods.
